BY-LAWS OF CMC KERALA ALUMNI TRUST
Dated: February 8, 2020

ARTICLE 1
DECLARATION OF TRUST AND PRINCIPAL OFFICE

Section 1.1: Declaration of Trust. These By-Laws are adopted pursuant to the Declaration of Trust, as from time to time in effect (the “Declaration”), of CMC Kerala Alumni Trust (the “Trust”) and shall be subject to the terms of the Declaration. Any capitalized term not otherwise defined herein shall have the same meaning given to such term in the Declaration.


Section 1.2: Principal Office of the Trust. The principal office of the Trust is Pynummoottil House, Perumthuruthy P.O., Tiruvalla, Kerala, 689107, or such other place as shall be determined by the Trustees from time to time.


Section 1.3: The term CMC in the name of the Trust and the General Body refers to the Christian Medical College, Vellore, Tamilnadu.


Section 1.4: The CMC Kerala Alumni Trust is a Private, Self-Governing, Not for Profit, Social Service Voluntary organization rendering service in the form of Free Medical Camps, Dissemination of Basic Health needs and Knowledge in the society irrespective of caste, creed or religion and is apolitical. It is registered under the Indian Trust Act, in the State of Kerala. (Article 5)




ARTICLE 2

TRUSTEES

Section 2.1: There shall be three Trustees, The President, The Secretary and The Treasurer.


Section 2.2: The number of the Trustees shall never be less than three.


Section 2.3: The number of Trustees maybe increased at any time as per majority decision taken during a General Body meeting of the CMC Kerala Alumni.


Section 2.4: The Board of Trustees, consisting of the President, the Secretary and the Treasurer (and/or any additional Member as per clause mentioned in Section 2.3), shall be elected by the General Body of the CMC Kerala Alumni at their Annual (Sections 4.12 and 7.2) meeting and shall hold office for a period of one year or till the the next General Body meeting or until their earlier death, resignation, removal or disqualification.

Section 2.5: The President shall ordinarily be the Chair (or, if the Chair is unable to attend any such meeting, the Chair’s designee) and shall preside at all meetings of the Trustees. The Chair shall have such other duties and powers as the Trustees may from time to time determine.


Section 2.6: Regular Meetings. Regular meetings of the Trustees may be held without call or notice at such places (including for these purposes, by means of conference telephone circuit, video conferencing or similar communications equipment by means of which all persons participating in the meeting can hear each other as provided for in the Declaration) as the Trustees may from time to time determine.


Section 2.7: Special Meetings. Special meetings of the Trustees may be held at any time and at any place (including for these purposes, by means of conference telephone circuit, video conferencing or similar communications equipment by means of which all persons participating in the meeting can hear each other as provided for in the Declaration) designated in the call of the meeting when called by the Chair, the President or the Secretary or by two or more Trustees, sufficient notice thereof as described in Section 2.4 below being given to each Trustee by the Secretary or an Assistant Secretary or by the person calling the meeting.


Section 2.8: Notice of Special Meetings. It shall be sufficient notice to a Trustee of a special meeting to send notice by overnight mail at least two days or by email or facsimile at least one day before the meeting addressed to the Trustee at his or her usual or last known business or residence address, email address or facsimile number as applicable, or to give notice in person or by telephone one day before the meeting. Notice may be sent on the day of the special meeting by email, facsimile or other electronic means, or given by telephone or in person, if under the circumstances the party calling the meeting deems more immediate action to be necessary or appropriate. Notice of a special meeting need not be given to any Trustee if a written waiver of notice, executed by him or her before or after the meeting, is filed with the records of the meeting, or to any Trustee who attends the meeting without protesting prior thereto or at its commencement the lack of notice to him or her. Neither notice of a meeting nor a waiver of a notice need specify the purposes of the meeting.


Section 2.9: Quorum and Manner of Acting. At any meeting of the Trustees a majority of the Trustees then in office shall constitute a quorum. Any meeting may be adjourned from time to time by a majority of the votes cast upon the question, whether or not a quorum is present, and the meeting may be held as adjourned without further notice. A meeting at which a quorum is initially present may continue to transact business notwithstanding the withdrawal from the meeting of one or more Trustees if any action taken is approved by at least a majority of the required quorum for that meeting. The Declaration contains further provisions relating to the manner of acting by the Trustees.


Section 2.10: Actions by Consent. If in accordance with the provisions of the Declaration any action is taken by the Trustees by a written consent of fewer than all of the Trustees, prompt notice of any such action shall be furnished to each Trustee who did not execute such written consent, provided that the effectiveness of such action shall not be impaired by any delay or failure to furnish such notice. Any written consents may be executed and delivered by electronic means. The Declaration contains further provisions relating to action by consent of the Trustees.


Section 2.11: Counsel and Experts. The Trustees who are not Interested Persons may, by vote of a majority of such Trustees, at the Trust’s expense, hire such employees and retain such counsel, accountants, appraisers or other experts or consultants whose services such Trustees may, in their discretion, determine to be necessary or desirable from time to time, including services to one or more committees established by the Trustees, and may execute any agreements, contracts, instruments or other documents in connection therewith.




ARTICLE 3

MEMBERS OF GENERAL BODY OF THE CMC KERALA ALUMNI

Section 3.1: The Membership of the General body of the CMC Kerala Alumni shall currently be limited to Alumni of the Christian Medical College, Vellore, Tamilnadu, who completed a course of studies in the said Institution leading to the University Degree of MBBS and are practicing or are resident in the State of Kerala.


Section 3.2: Membership to the General Body may subsequently be extended if decided upon by the General Body of the current Members, to persons who have completed a course of study at the Christian Medical College, Vellore, Tamil Nadu leading to a Postgraduate qualification in the field of Medicine.


Section 3.3: Membership to the General Body may subsequently be extended if decided upon by the General Body of the current Members to persons who have completed a course of study at the Christian Medical College, Vellore, Tamil Nadu leading to a qualification in the field of Nursing or in the Para-Medical and Allied Specialty fields.


Section 3.4: Membership to the General Body however will always be limited to Alumni of the Christian Medical College, Vellore, Tamil Nadu. This is absolute and unchangeable.


Section 3.5: Retired Teaching Staff of the said Christian Medical College, Vellore, Tamil Nadu, may from time to time be invited as special guests of the General Body to their Annual meetings.




ARTICLE 4

MEETINGS OF THE GENERAL BODY

Section 4.1: There shall be annual meetings of Members. At least one General Body meeting shall be held in a calendar year.


Section 4.2: Location of the next General Body meeting would normally be decided at the General Body meeting held in the previous year.


Section 4.3: The Annual meeting will be held on the 2nd Saturday of February every year unless this date is changed during an Annual General Body meeting. (See Section 4.8)


Section 4.4: The Trustees under exceptional circumstances may opt to change the time or the location of the meeting.


Section 4.5: All Meetings shall be conducted following Parliamentary Rules of Conduct.


Section 4.6: The President or his designated representative shall chair all General Body meetings of the CMC Kerala Alumni.


Section 4.7: Quorum. There need to be a required quorum at the Annual General Body meetings of the Membership of the CMC Kerala Alumni, which shall be not less than 20% of the current Total Registered Membership of the CMC Kerala Alumni.


Section 4.8: Changes in the Bylaws. However, to vote in any change in the existing Bylaws of the CMC Kerala Alumni, a Quorum of at least one third of the Registered Membership of the CMC Kerala Alumni shall be present.


Section 4.9: An Extraordinary meeting of the Members of the CMC Kerala Alumni may be called by the Secretary whenever ordered by (I) a majority of Trustees then in office, (ii) the Chair or (iii) the President. Meetings of the Members of the Trust or of any Series shall also be called by the Secretary upon the order of the Trustees upon the written request of the Members holding not less than one-third of the total registered Members entitled to vote on the matters specified in such written request provided that (1) such request shall state the purposes of such meeting and the matters proposed to be acted on. The Trustees may determine, or may authorize the officers of the Trust to determine, the date, time and place for any meeting of Members, which place may be within or outside the State of Kerala. Any meeting so called may be postponed or cancelled prior to the meeting, provided that notice of such postponement or cancellation is given to the Members entitled to vote at that meeting, if notice of such meeting had been given to Members.


Section 4.10: Notice of Meetings. Notice of all meetings of Members and any postponements thereof, stating the time, place and purposes of the meeting, shall be given by the Secretary or the Trustees hereof at least seven days and not more than 120 days before the date for the meeting set forth in such notice, to each Member of record. Any adjourned meeting may be held as adjourned without further notice, even if the date of such adjourned meeting is more than 120 days after the notice of the original meeting or the postponement thereof, was mailed or sent.


Section 4.11: Adjournments. Any meeting of Members may, by action of the person presiding thereat, be adjourned without further notice with respect to one or more matters to be considered at such meeting to a designated time and place, if a quorum is not present with respect to such matter. (Sections 4.7 and 9.2)


Section 4.12: Conduct of Meetings. For any matter to be properly discussed before any meeting of Members, the matter must be either specified in the notice of meeting given by or at the direction of a majority of the Trustees then in office or otherwise brought before the meeting by or at the direction of the Chair or other presiding officer.


Section 4.13: Election of Trustees. Trustees shall be elected by voice vote at the Annual General body meeting of the Membership (Sections 2.4 and 7.2)


Section 4.14: Communications with Members. Any notices, reports, statements or other communications with Members of any kind required under the Declaration, these By-Laws or applicable law may be sent, delivered or made available in any reasonable manner as may be determined by the Trustees if not otherwise prohibited by applicable law, including, without limitation, by email or other electronic means or by posting on a website; No communication need be given to any Member who shall have failed to inform the Trust of the Member’s current address.




ARTICLE 5

OBJECTIVES, FUNCTIONS AND PURPOSE OF EXISTENCE OF THE CMC KERALA ALUMNI

Section 5.1: The CMC Kerala Alumni Trust maintained to render service in the form of Free Medical Camps, Dissemination of Basic Health needs and Knowledge in the society irrespective of caste, creed or religion and is apolitical. (Section 1.4)


Section 5.2: One of the main Objectives of the creation of the CMC Kerala Alumni is for like minded persons who trained in one of the foremost Institutions for Medical training in the country to get together, exchange ideas, collaborate and discuss matters of importance in the health field.


Section 5.3: The CMC Kerala Alumni will continue to maintain regular contact with their Alma Mater, the Christian Medical College, Vellore, Tamilnadu throughout their existence.


Section 5.4: The CMC Kerala Alumni intends to collaborate on Multi-centric research with the Alma mater, which is well known for its academic activities.


Section 5.5: Through collaborative work we the Members of the CMC Kerala Alumni hope to to improve the health and welfare of the people we serve – keeping in mind our CMC motto of “Not to be Ministered Unto but to Minister” in its totality of Ministering




ARTICLE 6

COMMITTEES

Section 6.1: Establishment and Authority. The Declaration contains provisions relating to the establishment of committees by the Trustees. Each committee shall have such powers and authority as shall be authorized by the Trustees, and may fix its own rules and procedures, and adopt its own charter, in each case subject to approval by the Trustees. The Trustees may abolish any such committee at any time in their sole discretion. Any committee to which the Trustees delegate any of their powers shall maintain records of its meetings and shall report its actions to the Trustees. The Trustees shall have the power to rescind any action of any committee, but no such rescission shall have retroactive effect. The Trustees shall have the power at any time to fill vacancies in the committees. The Trustees may designate one or more Trustees as alternate members of any committee who may replace any absent member at any meeting of the committee.


Section 6.2: Quorum; Voting. Unless the specific rules and procedures adopted by a committee provide otherwise, a majority of the members of any committee of the Trustees shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business, and any action of such a committee may be taken at a meeting by a vote of a majority of the members present (a quorum being present) or evidenced by one or more writings signed by such a majority (which writings may be executed and/or delivered by electronic means). Members of a committee may participate in a meeting of such committee by means of a conference telephone circuit, video conferencing or similar communications equipment by means of which all persons participating in the meeting can hear each other at the same time and participation by such means shall constitute presence in person at a meeting.


Section 6.3: Compensation of Members. The chair, if any, and members of any duly appointed committee shall not receive such compensation and/or fees and reimbursement other than for expenses incurred on behalf of work undertaken for the trust as from time to time may be determined by the Trustees.




ARTICLE 7

OFFICERS

Section 7.1: Enumeration; Qualification. The officers of the Trust shall be a President, a Treasurer, a Secretary, and such other officers as the Trustees from time to time may in their discretion elect, appoint or authorize in accordance with Section 6.2 below. Any officer of the Trust may but need not be a Trustee. Any two or more offices, except those of President and Vice-President, may be held by the same person.


Section 7.2: Election. The President, the Treasurer and the Secretary shall be elected by the General body upon the occurrence of a vacancy in any such office. Other officers, if any, may be elected or appointed by the Trustees at any time, or the Trustees may delegate to the President the power to appoint such other officers as the Trustees shall at any time or from time to time deem advisable. Vacancies in any such other office may be filled at any time. Each officer shall hold office at the pleasure of the Trustees.


Section 7.3: Powers. Subject to the other provisions of these By-Laws, each officer shall have, in addition to the duties and powers herein and in the Declaration set forth, such duties and powers as are commonly incident to the office occupied by him or her as if the Trust were organized as a Kerala statutory trust and such other duties and powers as the Trustees may from time to time designate.


Section 7.4: President. Unless the Trustees otherwise provide, the President shall be the chief executive officer of the Trust.


Section 7.5: Treasurer. Unless the Trustees provide otherwise, the Treasurer shall be the chief financial and accounting officer of the Trust, and shall, subject to the provisions of the Declaration and to any arrangement made by the Trustees with a custodian, investment adviser or manager, or transfer, shareholder servicing or similar agent, be in charge of the valuable papers, books of account and accounting records of the Trust, and shall have such other duties and powers as may be designated from time to time by the Trustees or by the President.


Section 7.6: Secretary. The Secretary shall record all proceedings of the Shareholders and the Trustees in books to be kept therefor. In the absence of the Secretary from any meeting of the shareholders or Trustees, an Assistant Secretary, or if there be none or if he or she is absent, a temporary Secretary chosen at such meeting shall record the proceedings thereof in the aforesaid books.


Section 7.7: Resignations and Removals. Any officer may resign at any time by written instrument signed by him or her and delivered to the Chair, the President or the Secretary or to a meeting of the Trustees. Such resignation shall be effective upon receipt unless specified to be effective at some other time. The Trustees may by action of a majority of the Trustees then in office, remove any officer with or without cause. Except to the extent expressly provided in a written agreement with the Trust, no officer resigning and no officer removed shall have any right to any compensation for any period following his or her resignation or removal, or any right to damages on account of such removal.




ARTICLE 8

MISCELLANEOUS

Section 8.1: Books and Records. The books and records of the Trust and any Series thereof, including the share ledger or ledgers, may be kept in or outside the State of Kerala at such office or offices of the Trust and/or its agents as may from time to time be determined by the officers of the Trust.


Section 8.2: Access to Book and Records. The Members shall only have such right to inspect the records, documents, accounts and books of the Trust or any Series thereof as may be granted from time to time by the Trustees in their sole discretion.


Section 8.3: Seal. The Trustees may adopt a seal of the Trust which shall be in such form and shall have such inscription thereon as the Trustees may from time to time prescribe, but unless otherwise required by the Trustees, the seal shall not be necessary to be placed on, and its absence shall not impair the validity of, any document, instrument or other paper executed and delivered by or on behalf of the Trust.


Section 8.4: Management of Funds. All funds accrued by the Membership of the CMC Kerala Alumni shall be maintained in a Bank Account opened in the Name of the CMC Kerala Alumni in a Bank suitable for the purpose by the Trustees.


Section 8.5: Operation of the Bank account shall be carried out by at least two signatories of the three Trustees.


Section 8.6: Execution of Papers. All deeds, leases, contracts, notes and other obligations made by the Trustees shall be signed by the President, any Vice President elected by the Trustees, the Treasurer, the Secretary or any other officer elected by the Trustees, except as the Trustees may generally or in particular cases authorize the execution thereof in some other manner, or as otherwise provided in these By-Laws.


Section 8.7: Severability. The provisions of these By-Laws are severable. If the Trustees determine, with the advice of counsel, that any provision hereof conflicts with the Kerala Trust Act. If any provision hereof shall be held invalid or unenforceable in any jurisdiction, such invalidity or unenforceability shall attach only to such provision only in such jurisdiction and shall not affect any other provision of these By-Laws.


Section 8.8: Headings. Headings are placed in these By-Laws for convenience of reference only and in case of any conflict, the text of these By-Laws rather than the headings shall control.




ARTICLE 9

AMENDMENTS TO THE BYLAWS

Section 9.1: General.These By-Laws may be amended, supplemented and restated, or repealed, in whole or in part, by a majority of the Trustees then in office at any meeting of the Trustees, or by one or more writings signed by such a majority.


Section 9.2: The amendments to the Bylaws has then to be presented at the Annual General Body meeting and has to be passed by the General Body before it can come into effect. (Section 4.7)
